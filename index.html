I am a PhD candidate in economics at ESSEC and a member of THEMA, a research center jointly affiliated with CY Cergy Paris Université, ESSEC, and CNRS. I work in applied microeconomics at the intersection of migration, education, identity, and family. My research studies how legal status, historical institutions, and hostile social environments shape belonging, discrimination, and human capital formation.

Borders Within: Citizenship and the Paradox of Belonging for Turkish-Origin Youth

This paper studies the effects of Germany’s 2000 Nationality Act on second-generation Turkish-origin youth. Using a difference-in-differences design around birthright citizenship eligibility, I examine whether citizenship expands belonging and educational integration, or whether it can also heighten perceived exclusion in selective institutional settings.
